KEY DIFFERENCES FROM CURRENT:

1. **Brand becomes storytelling**
   - Not just "here's the logo" but "why we designed it this way"
   - History, evolution, philosophy
   - External audience focus (partners, press, community)

2. **Components stay practical**
   - Engineers get code
   - Designers get Figma usage
   - No brand philosophy here - just "how to use"

3. **Resources consolidates guidance**
   - All "how to design with Origin" lives here
   - Patterns, accessibility, content, Figma usage
   - Designer-focused practical content

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

INSPIRATION: design.cash.app

Their structure separates:
- Brand story (why things are the way they are)
- Practical tools (components, downloads)
- Guidelines (how to use everything correctly)

This creates clear audience paths:
- External stakeholders → Brand
- Engineers → Components
- Designers → Components + Resources
- Everyone → specific section for their needs

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

IMPLEMENTATION CONSIDERATIONS:

✅ **Pros:**
- Clearer audience segmentation
- Brand becomes more compelling (storytelling vs rules)
- Resources consolidates designer guidance
- Easier to maintain (clear content boundaries)

⚠️ **Challenges:**
- Significant content reorganization
- Need actual brand history/philosophy content
- Current content is mostly practical, not storytelling
- Requires dedicated content writer for brand stories

⏰ **Timeline:**
- Phase 1: Current simplified structure (in progress)
- Phase 2: Stabilize and add missing practical content
- Phase 3: Develop brand storytelling content
- Phase 4: Reorganize into this future structure
